Alfred Hitchcock

Sir Alfred Joseph Hitchcock was a renowned film director and producer whom has influenced the film industry through his storytelling and talented use of mise en scene in many movies within the thriller genre. He lived from August 13th 1899 to April 29th 1980 and was well-known for his films such as Birds and Vertigo which at the time were well received by audiences and equals within the film industry. Alfred Hitchcock had a theme in his movies of psychologically taking his audiences through a suspenseful adventure that kept them on edege at every moment. Throughout his film career he directed over 50 films, leaving him to be one of the most highly regarded producers and directors in England and Hollywood over his career of almost 6 decades.

Two movies that are still iconic and resonate with audiences are "the Birds" and "Virtigo". both suspense thriller movies that engage audiences and inspire Holloywood producers and directors today.
