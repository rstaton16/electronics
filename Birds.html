Alfred Hitchcock's: BIRDS

Alfred Hitchcock

The Birds directed by Alfred Hitchcock and released in 1963, is one of the most iconic and influential horror movies in the film industry to date. The movie takes apart the unknown suspense and horror to the audience of sudden and fatal bird attacks in the California coast area. The film details the lengths that people took to get away from the birds who attacked them with lethal force. The film shows a frenzy of different groups at the beginning trying to avoid the bird attacks and investigating why the birds were acting in a peculiar manner. As the film progresses the shock and awe factor is taken in and multiplied by the number of on scene incidences of massive flocks of birds attacking and imprisoning people to safety. The film in a nutshell without being spoiled is built on the suspense of never knowing if the birds will continue to attack or when they will stop.
